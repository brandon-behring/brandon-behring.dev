## The decision to think through

Three directions compete for "next." This is a genuine portfolio-strategy fork —
surface it to Brandon rather than assuming:

**1. Deepen the A4 demo (the arc we just set up).** Two sub-steps, in order:

   - **1a — Overlay consolidation** (self-contained, no external deps):
     `rl_and_control#1` + `brandon-behring.dev#3` are *the same arc* — fold the 8
     hand-curated overrides (Kalman ×2, Watkins, Sutton & Barto, Bertsekas) **and
     the DPG fix** (arXiv `1406.2199` is actually "Two-Stream Convolutional
     Networks"; DPG has no arXiv id) into `rl_and_control/references/paper_index.md`
     so `build_graph_export.py` emits them — then **delete `enrich-citation-graph.mjs`**.
     Result: the site is a *literal* pure consumer; the "generated by my pipeline"
     caption becomes 100% true. Medium effort, touches rl_and_control + this repo.
   - **1b — Densification** (the *visible* payoff): add pre-arXiv classics +
     textbooks (~40 `[needed]` rows in `paper_index.md`: Bellman 1957, Pontryagin
     1962, Baird 1995, …). **Pipeline is ready** (#16/#20 done) — the bottleneck is
     **corpus acquisition**: arXiv `[needed]` papers need PDFs ingested; the
     classics are books → `research-kb sources add-manual` + role-tag
     `anchor.rl_optimal_control` (mirror the existing anchors). Then re-extract +
     `--rebuild-unmatched` (now crash-safe) + re-export. Effort is dominated by
     acquisition, which is uncertain/manual.

   *Sequencing:* 1a first (cheap, completes provenance, no deps), then 1b as papers
   are acquired. OR skip straight to 1b if you want the visible win and are willing
   to do acquisition.
